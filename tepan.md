# Frontier Toki Pona (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ)

## Relative Clauses

A ꜱᴜʙᴏʀᴅɪɴᴀᴛᴇ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ can be a question with **seme**, yielding a ʀᴇʟᴀᴛɪᴠᴇ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ.

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) o toki e ni: mani li lon seme. ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) mani li lon seme? o toki e ni. |
|:-|
| *Tell me where the money is.* |

When there is enough context, a [ʀᴇʟᴀᴛɪᴠᴇ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ](#subordinate-clause) can be reduced to its **predicate**, and the **direct object** can be omitted.

| (ᴘᴜ) jan ni li tawa musi lon poka pi soweli alasa. ona li ... ⇒ (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) jan ni: tawa musi lon poka pi soweli alasa, li ... |
|:-|
| *the person that is dancing with wolves* |

| (ᴘᴜ) mi sona e jan ni: ona li ... ⇒ (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) jan ni: mi sona, li ... |
|:-|
| *the person that I know* |

## Passive and Causative

There is no ᴘᴀꜱꜱɪᴠᴇ.

ᴛʀᴀɴꜱɪᴛɪᴠᴇ ᴠᴇʀʙs used as ɴᴏᴜɴs omit **ijo**. Without omitting **ijo**, ᴘᴀꜱꜱɪᴠᴇ can be expressed.

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) kala li ijo moku lon soweli. ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) soweli li moku e kala. |
|:-|
| *The fish is being eaten by the cat.* |

Using **jan** instead of **ijo**, ᴄᴀᴜꜱᴀᴛɪᴠᴇ can be expressed.

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) mi jan moku e soweli lon kala. ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) mi kama e ni: soweli li moku e kala. |
|:-|
| *I made the cat eat the fish.* |


---

This document treats some ᴘᴏɴᴀ rules as ᴏᴘᴛɪᴏɴᴀʟ stylistic advices, when they introduce additional grammatical concepts merely for the sake of a simpler style. For example, the distinction between [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ᴘʜʀᴀꜱᴇ](#noun)s and [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ɢʀᴏᴜᴘ](#noun)s only serves the purpose of motivating the ᴘᴏɴᴀ rule that [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ᴘʜʀᴀꜱᴇ](#noun)s can't contain more than one ["pi"](#genitive). In case of doubt: be ᴘᴏɴᴀ!

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) nimi pi jan pona, pi toki pona ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) nimi ni pi jan pona li lon toki pona: ona |
|:-|
| *a friend's Toki Pona name* |

A (simple) [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ᴘʜʀᴀꜱᴇ](#noun) consists of a [ɴᴏᴜɴ](#noun) with any number of [ᴀᴅᴊᴇᴄᴛɪᴠᴇ](#adjective)s (including zero). The ᴘᴀʀᴛɪᴄʟᴇ **"pi"** goes between the first and the second (simple) [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ᴘʜʀᴀꜱᴇ](#noun) (the second one having one or more [ᴀᴅᴊᴇᴄᴛɪᴠᴇ](#adjective)s). Therefore, a [ɴᴏᴜɴ-ᴘʜʀᴀꜱᴇ](#noun) must not contain more than one **"pi"**-phrase, so neither subsequent nor nested **"pi"**-phrases are possible.

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) ona li jo e tomo pi telo nasa pi ma ante. ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) ona li jo e telo pi telo nasa. telo nasa li tan ma ante. |
|:-|
| *She has a house of imported beer.* ⇒ *She has a bar. The beer is imported.* |

[ꜱᴏɴᴊᴀ](sonja.md#lesson-17) always completes the ᴍᴀɪɴ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ before beginning the ꜱᴜʙᴏʀᴅɪɴᴀᴛᴇ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ, even if the ᴍᴀɪɴ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ doesn't end with the word described or modified by the ꜱᴜʙᴏʀᴅɪɴᴀᴛᴇ ᴄʟᴀᴜꜱᴇ.

| (ᴛᴇᴘᴀɴ) jan Isa li toki e ni: "mama mi o!" tawa sewi. ⇒ (ᴘᴏɴᴀ) jan Isa li toki e ni tawa sewi: "mama mi o!" |
|:-|
| Jesus prayed: "My father!" |
